BrowserBouncer

Privacy Policy

Effective 26 April 2026 · Last updated 26 April 2026

The short version

BrowserBouncer does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data — ever. All routing rules live only on your Mac. No analytics, no accounts, no tracking.

📋

Overview

BrowserBouncer is a macOS menu bar application that intercepts URL opening events and routes them to a browser of your choice based on rules you configure. This privacy policy explains what information the app handles and how.

🚫

No Data Collection

BrowserBouncer does not collect any personal information. The app does not transmit any data to external servers, third parties, or the developer. There are no analytics libraries, no crash reporting services, and no advertising SDKs included in the application.

💾

Local Storage Only

Your routing rules — the app-to-browser mappings you define — are stored exclusively in your Mac's local app storage using macOS standard data persistence (UserDefaults). This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to anyone other than you.

The URLs that BrowserBouncer intercepts are processed in memory for routing purposes only and are not logged, stored, or transmitted anywhere.

🌐

No Internet Access Required

BrowserBouncer does not require an internet connection to function. The app makes no outbound network requests of any kind during normal operation.

🔒

macOS Permissions

BrowserBouncer is set as your default browser handler at the operating system level so it can intercept link-opening events. This is a standard macOS mechanism. The app does not use this permission to monitor your browsing activity — it only reads the URL at the moment a link is opened in order to route it immediately.

👶

Children's Privacy

BrowserBouncer does not collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. There is nothing to collect.

🔄

Changes to This Policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a revised effective date. Given the nature of the app, we do not anticipate meaningful changes.
